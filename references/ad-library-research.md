# Meta Ad Library & Competitive Research Reference

## What Is the Meta Ad Library?

**URL: https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/**

A free, public transparency tool by Meta showing every active ad running across Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, Threads, WhatsApp, and the Audience Network. No account required to browse. Originally launched in 2019 for political ad transparency after the Cambridge Analytica scandal, it has evolved into one of the most powerful competitive intelligence tools available to marketers.

## When to Use the Ad Library

**Always use the Ad Library when:**
- A user asks for creative inspiration or "what's working" in their niche
- Researching competitor ad strategies
- Checking what ad formats are dominant in a specific industry
- Planning seasonal campaigns (check what competitors ran last year)
- Validating creative concepts before production
- Exploring new markets or verticals
- Identifying messaging gaps or opportunities
- Checking if a competitor is actively advertising (and how aggressively)

**Proactively suggest Ad Library research when:**
- User is launching a new campaign and needs inspiration
- User is entering a new market
- User's performance is declining (check if competitors changed strategy)
- User is unsure about creative formats to test
- User asks "what kind of ads should I run?"

## How to Search the Ad Library

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Go to** https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/
2. **Select location/country:** Choose the TARGET market, not where you're based. E.g., if targeting Italy, select Italy.
3. **Select ad category:** For most commercial research, choose "All Ads." Use special categories (Issues, Elections, Politics) only for those specific types.
4. **Search by:**
   - **Brand/Page name:** Direct competitor lookup (most precise)
   - **Keyword:** Industry terms, product categories, or specific phrases (broader discovery)

### Available Filters
- **Platform:** Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, Audience Network, WhatsApp, Threads
- **Media type:** Image, Video, Carousel, Meme (helps isolate format strategies)
- **Active status:** Active ads only, or include inactive/completed
- **Impressions by date:** Filter by time range
- **Language:** Useful for multi-market research
- **Ad status badges:** "Low impression count" badge (< 100 impressions) indicates tests or poor performers

### What You Can See Per Ad
- Full creative (image, video, carousel)
- Ad copy (primary text, headline, description)
- CTA button type
- Ad start date (critical for longevity analysis)
- Platforms where it runs
- **EU ads additionally show:** Age targeting, gender targeting, location targeting, estimated reach, estimated spend

## Competitive Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify Your Competitors
- Direct competitors (same product/service)
- Indirect competitors (adjacent solutions)
- Aspirational brands (larger brands in your space you want to emulate)
- Search both by brand name AND by product keywords

### Step 2: Analyze Creative Strategy

**Format analysis:**
- What % of their ads are video vs. image vs. carousel?
- Are they using UGC-style content or polished brand creative?
- Do they use different formats for different platforms?
- Are they testing Reels-specific vertical video?

**Copy analysis:**
- What hooks do they use in the first line?
- What CTAs are most common?
- Do they use social proof (numbers, testimonials)?
- What tone of voice (professional, casual, urgent)?
- How long is their primary text?

**Offer analysis:**
- What value propositions lead their messaging?
- Are they discount-driven, value-driven, or benefit-driven?
- What lead magnets do they offer (free consultation, guide, discount)?
- How do they handle pricing (shown, hidden, "from €X")?

### Step 3: Identify Winners (The Longevity Signal)

**The single most important insight from the Ad Library: ad longevity.**

- **If an ad has been running for weeks or months, it's profitable.** Period. No business pays to run a losing ad for 3+ months.
- **Long-running ads = proven winners** worth studying and drawing inspiration from
- **Short-lived ads (days)** = tests or poor performers
- **Many short-lived ads from one brand** = active testing phase (they haven't found a winner yet)
- **A mix of long-running + new ads** = healthy brand testing new concepts while scaling winners

**How to spot winners:**
- Check the "Started running" date — older = better performing
- Ads running 30+ days are likely profitable
- Ads running 90+ days are definite winners
- Cross-reference with number of active ads (fewer long-running > many short-running)

### Step 4: Track Timing & Seasonal Patterns

- Check ad start dates around key periods: BFCM, EOFY, summer, back-to-school, holidays
- Note when competitors start seasonal pushes (often weeks before you'd expect)
- Identify campaign cycle patterns (monthly refreshes, quarterly pushes)
- Plan your campaigns to either compete directly or find gaps

### Step 5: Identify Messaging Gaps & Opportunities

- If ALL competitors focus on "affordability," differentiate with quality/speed/premium
- If competitors are only on Instagram, test Facebook-heavy placements
- If they're all running static images, video could stand out
- If no one targets your specific sub-niche, you have a messaging gap to exploit
- Look for what's MISSING in competitor ads, not just what's there

## Practical Research Workflows

### Quick Competitor Check (5-10 minutes)
1. Search 3-5 direct competitors by name
2. Note: How many active ads? What formats? What offers?
3. Identify their longest-running ads
4. Screenshot or save the best creative for reference

### Deep Competitive Audit (30-60 minutes)
1. Research 10+ competitors (direct, indirect, aspirational)
2. For each: catalog format mix, copy themes, offers, CTAs
3. Build a spreadsheet tracking: brand, format, hook, offer, longevity, platform
4. Identify 3-5 patterns across all competitors
5. Find 2-3 gaps or underserved angles
6. Create a brief for new creative concepts based on findings

### Ongoing Monitoring (Weekly, 15 minutes)
1. Check top 5 competitors for new ads
2. Note any format/messaging shifts
3. Flag long-running ads that are NEW (competitor found a new winner)
4. Track when competitors pause or stop campaigns
5. Adjust your own strategy based on competitive shifts

### Pre-Campaign Research
1. Search your product category keywords
2. Filter by target country and platform
3. Sort/browse for longest-running ads
4. Analyze winning creative patterns (format, hook, CTA, visual style)
5. Identify what's proven to work before you invest in production
6. Use findings to brief your creative team

## Advanced Tips & Tricks

### Finding Page IDs for Precise Tracking
- Searching by exact Page name can miss results
- Use "Find my FB ID" tools to get the numeric Page ID
- Search by Page ID for most accurate results

### EU Data Advantage
- EU ads must disclose additional targeting data
- You can see age ranges, gender splits, and geographic targeting
- This reveals competitors' audience strategy — invaluable intelligence
- Even if you're not in the EU, search EU competitors to see this data

### Low Impression Badge
- Meta now shows a "Low impression count" badge on ads with <100 impressions
- These are creative tests or underperformers
- Useful for seeing what competitors are TESTING (even if it's not working yet)

### Cross-Platform Comparison
- Filter by Facebook vs. Instagram to see if competitors use different strategies per platform
- Many brands run testimonial videos on Facebook but short-form Reels on Instagram
- This reveals platform-specific creative insights

### Seasonal Archives
- Search for competitors' past campaigns by filtering date ranges
- E.g., filter November-December to see previous holiday campaigns
- Use historical patterns to plan your upcoming seasonal strategy

### WhatsApp & Threads Ads
- You can now filter specifically for WhatsApp and Threads ads
- These are newer placements — if competitors aren't using them, it could be an early-mover opportunity

## What the Ad Library DOESN'T Show

Important limitations to communicate to users:

- ❌ **No performance data:** You can't see CTR, ROAS, CPA, or conversion rates
- ❌ **No audience targeting details** (unless EU)
- ❌ **No budget/spend data** (unless EU, and even then it's estimated)
- ❌ **No A/B test results:** You can see variants but not which won
- ❌ **No landing page data:** You see the ad but not the full funnel (click through manually to check)
- ❌ **No historical archive** for non-political ads (only currently active + recently stopped)

**The proxy for performance = longevity.** Long-running ads are profitable ads. This is the most reliable competitive signal available.

## Tools That Enhance Ad Library Research

### Built-in API Scripts (Bundled with This Skill)

This skill includes Facebook's official Ad Library API Python scripts from `facebookresearch/Ad-Library-API-Script-Repository`. These scripts are located in the `scripts/` directory and allow programmatic access to the Ad Library data.

**⚠️ IMPORTANT: Before using any script, you MUST ask the user for their Facebook Developer Access Token.** The token is required for all API calls. Ask:

> "To query the Meta Ad Library API, I need your Facebook Developer access token. You can get one at https://developers.facebook.com/tools/accesstoken/. Please provide it so I can run the query."

**Never hardcode or store the token.** Always receive it from the user at runtime.

#### Requirements
```bash
pip install requests iso3166
```

#### Available Scripts

**1. `scripts/fb_ads_library_api.py`** — Core API module
- `FbAdsLibraryTraversal` class handles paginated API requests
- Parameters: `access_token`, `fields`, `search_term`, `country`, `search_page_ids`, `ad_active_status`, `after_date`, `page_limit`, `api_version`
- Handles retry logic and date filtering automatically
- Returns a generator of ad archive batches

**2. `scripts/fb_ads_library_api_cli.py`** — Command-line interface
- Full CLI for querying the Ad Library API

Usage examples:
```bash
# Count all active ads for a page in Italy
python scripts/fb_ads_library_api_cli.py \
  -t {ACCESS_TOKEN} \
  -f 'page_id,page_name,ad_creative_bodies,ad_delivery_start_time,ad_snapshot_url' \
  -c 'IT' \
  --search-page-ids '{PAGE_ID}' \
  --ad-active-status 'ACTIVE' \
  -v count

# Save competitor ads to CSV
python scripts/fb_ads_library_api_cli.py \
  -t {ACCESS_TOKEN} \
  -f 'page_id,page_name,ad_creative_bodies,ad_creative_link_titles,ad_delivery_start_time,ad_delivery_stop_time,ad_snapshot_url,publisher_platforms,impressions,spend' \
  -c 'IT' \
  -s 'fisioterapista' \
  --ad-active-status 'ALL' \
  -v save_to_csv output.csv

# Get ads started after a specific date
python scripts/fb_ads_library_api_cli.py \
  -t {ACCESS_TOKEN} \
  -f 'page_name,ad_creative_bodies,ad_delivery_start_time,spend,impressions' \
  -c 'IT' \
  -s 'barbiere' \
  --after-date '2025-01-01' \
  -v save_to_csv barber_ads.csv

# Track ad start date trends to CSV
python scripts/fb_ads_library_api_cli.py \
  -t {ACCESS_TOKEN} \
  -f 'ad_delivery_start_time' \
  -c 'IT' \
  -s 'prenotazione online' \
  -v start_time_trending trend_output.csv
```

**3. `scripts/fb_ads_library_api_operators.py`** — Output operators
- `count` — Count total ads matching query
- `save` — Save results as JSON lines file
- `save_to_csv` — Save results as CSV (most useful for analysis)
- `start_time_trending` — Output ad count by start date (spot campaign timing patterns)

**4. `scripts/fb_ads_library_api_utils.py`** — Utilities
- Country code validation (supports IT, US, GB, DE, FR, ES, and 26 other countries)
- Field validation against the API's supported fields

#### Using the API Programmatically (Custom Scripts)

When the user needs a custom analysis, you can write Python scripts that import from the bundled modules:

```python
import sys
sys.path.insert(0, 'path/to/skill/scripts')
from fb_ads_library_api import FbAdsLibraryTraversal

# Initialize the API
api = FbAdsLibraryTraversal(
    access_token="USER_TOKEN_HERE",
    fields="page_name,ad_creative_bodies,ad_delivery_start_time,spend,impressions",
    search_term="your keyword",
    country="IT",
    ad_active_status="ACTIVE",
    after_date="2025-01-01",
    api_version="v21.0"  # Use latest API version
)

# Iterate through results
for batch in api.generate_ad_archives():
    for ad in batch:
        print(ad["page_name"], ad.get("ad_creative_bodies", []))
```

#### Practical Use Cases for the API Scripts

1. **Competitor audit:** Pull all active ads from a competitor's Page ID, export to CSV, analyze creative patterns and longevity

## Key Advice When Using Ad Library Insights

1. **Use as inspiration, never copy directly.** Replicate strategy and patterns, not creative assets.
2. **Context matters.** A winning ad for Nike may fail for a small local business — adapt to your brand and budget.
3. **Longevity = success signal.** Always prioritize studying long-running ads.
4. **Combine with your own data.** Ad Library shows what competitors do; your own Ads Manager shows what works for YOUR audience.
5. **Look for patterns across multiple competitors.** One brand's approach is an anecdote; a pattern across 5+ brands is a trend.
6. **Act on gaps, not just trends.** The biggest opportunities are often in what competitors AREN'T doing.
