# Policies & Compliance Reference

## Meta's Ad Review System

Every ad submitted is reviewed against Meta's Advertising Standards before approval:
- Automated AI systems catch >90% of violative ads in <2 minutes
- Manual review for edge cases
- Landing page also checked for consistency with ad
- Page quality and user response patterns monitored
- Repeated violations lead to escalating penalties

## Prohibited Content (Never Allowed)

These categories are completely banned across all Meta platforms:

1. **Illegal products and services** — drugs, illegal materials, weapons, counterfeit documents, any illegal activities
2. **Tobacco and related products** — cigarettes, vapes, nicotine-containing products
3. **Drugs and drug-related content** — recreational/prescription drugs, drug paraphernalia, misleading treatment claims
4. **Unsafe supplements** — unapproved weight loss products, steroids, controversial supplements
5. **Adult content** — sexually explicit imagery, pornography, adult entertainment services
6. **Weapons and ammunition** — sales, modifications, accessories
7. **Deceptive practices** — scams, misleading claims designed to extract money or personal information
8. **Misinformation** — content debunked by third-party fact-checkers; repeated offenders face advertising restrictions
9. **Shocking and violent content** — graphic violence, self-harm, cruelty, disturbing images
10. **Discriminatory practices** — targeting or content based on race, ethnicity, color, national origin, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, family status, disability
11. **Surveillance or stalking tools**
12. **Payday loans or paycheck advances** (in certain regions)
13. **Multi-level marketing** (with misleading income claims)

## Restricted Content (Allowed with Conditions)

These categories require extra care, documentation, disclosures, or pre-approval:

### Alcohol
- Must comply with local age restrictions and laws
- Cannot encourage people to drink
- Must target appropriate age groups
- Cannot show excessive consumption

### Gambling and Online Gaming
- Requires prior written permission from Meta
- Must target only legal jurisdictions
- Age restrictions apply

### Health and Wellness (Major 2025 Changes)
- **No body shaming** or exploiting insecurities
- **No before/after transformation images** for weight loss or anti-aging (exception: fitness classes like Pilates)
- **No close-up images** of pinched fat or specific body areas
- **No skin whitening/bleaching** products that cause permanent color change
- **No claims** to cure incurable/terminal illnesses (cancer, HIV, diabetes, autism, Alzheimer's, etc.)
- **No guaranteed results** within set timeframes without disclaimers
- **January 2025 restriction:** Health/wellness brands may lose access to Purchase/Add to Cart optimization events
  - Affected brands must use Landing Page Views or Engagement for optimization
  - Check Events Manager for classification status
  - Appeal if misclassified
  - Workarounds: custom-coded events, server-side tracking, native lead forms

### Financial Products and Services
- Must follow strict laws and advertising rules
- Additional verification may be required
- Cryptocurrency ads: varying restrictions by region, often require authorization
- No misleading financial claims

### Political/Social Issue Advertising
- Requires "Paid for by" disclaimers
- Advertiser verification mandatory
- Some content prohibited by local law ahead of voting
- EU: Additional transparency requirements under Digital Services Act
- Note: Meta may restrict political ads in certain regions

### Pharmaceuticals and Healthcare
- Must comply with local pharmaceutical advertising laws
- Prescription drugs: restrictions vary by country
- Online pharmacies: may require certification
- No unapproved drug claims

### Adult Products and Reproductive Health
- Must target users 18+
- No content focused on sexual pleasure
- Family planning and reproductive health education generally permitted with age targeting

## Special Ad Categories

When your ad falls into one of these categories, you MUST declare it:

### Housing
- Cannot target by age, gender, or zip code
- Limited interest targeting
- Must use Special Ad Category designation

### Employment
- Same targeting restrictions as housing
- Cannot discriminate based on protected characteristics

### Credit / Financial Services
- Restricted targeting similar to housing/employment
- Additional disclosures required

### Social Issues, Elections, and Politics
- "Paid for by" disclaimer required
- Advertiser identity verification
- Ad Library transparency

**Important:** Failure to declare a Special Ad Category when required can result in ad rejection or account restriction. When in doubt, declare it.

## Creative Compliance Triggers

Things that commonly get ads flagged or rejected by Meta's AI:

### Words/Phrases to Avoid or Use Carefully
- "Guaranteed" or "instant results" — only if verifiable
- "Best" or "number one" — needs substantiation
- "Cure" or "treatment" — for health products, extremely restricted
- "Before/after" language — triggers health policy review
- Excessive use of ALL CAPS or exclamation marks

### Visual Triggers
- Before/after comparison images (especially body-related)
- Close-up body images suggesting insecurity
- Implied nudity or overly suggestive imagery
- Graphic violence or shocking content
- Excessive text overlay on images (still affects delivery even though hard 20% rule was removed)

### Landing Page Requirements
- Must match the ad's promises and messaging
- Must load quickly
- Must include updated privacy policy
- Cannot be a dead link or error page
- Cannot be deceptive or misleading
- Cannot auto-download files

## What to Do When Ads Get Rejected

### Step-by-Step Process
1. **Read the rejection reason** in Ads Manager (hover over status)
2. **Check the specific policy** cited
3. **Fix the issue:**
   - If creative: modify the offending element
   - If copy: rewrite flagged language
   - If landing page: update the destination
4. **Resubmit** the ad or create a modified version
5. **Appeal if wrongly rejected:** Account Quality → Request Review
6. **Duplicate and modify trick:** Sometimes duplicating the ad with minor changes (a word or two) gets it past the AI

### Common False Flags
- Testimonials incorrectly flagged as drug/pharmaceutical content
- General wellness content flagged as health claims
- Product images flagged as adult content
- Brand names containing flagged keywords

### Prevention Tips
- Review Meta's ad guidelines before creating ads
- Match landing page with ad promise
- Use transparent, clear disclaimers where required
- Monitor Account Quality in Business Manager
- Avoid frequent violations — Meta tracks repeat offenders
- Check for updates every 2-6 months (policies change often)

## 2025 Policy Updates

### AI-Generated Content
- Mandatory "AI-generated" labels for synthetic content
- Applies to AI-generated images, video, and audio in ads

### EU Digital Services Act
- Enhanced transparency rules for EU advertisers
- Additional ad library requirements
- Stricter data-sharing restrictions

### Lead Ads (October 2025 Update)
- Data collected through lead forms must be used ONLY for the declared purpose
- Quote request data cannot be reused for newsletters without new consent
- Advertiser is fully responsible as data controller (GDPR compliance)
- Must include clear, up-to-date privacy notice
- Segment contacts by collection purpose
- Retain proof of consent
- Meta can suspend lead forms if inconsistencies detected (preventive, not punitive)

### iOS Considerations
- 30% fee for in-app boosting on iOS devices
- Always manage ads on desktop or non-iOS devices to avoid this fee
- App Tracking Transparency (ATT) opt-out continues to affect Pixel tracking
- CAPI is the solution for iOS tracking gaps

## Account Health Best Practices

1. **Monitor Account Quality** in Business Manager regularly
2. **Don't create multiple accounts** for the same business/offer
3. **Respond to rejections promptly** — accumulating rejected ads hurts account standing
4. **Maintain consistent, quality landing pages**
5. **Follow all local laws** in addition to Meta's policies
6. **Keep ad relevance high** — low-quality ads with poor engagement can affect account health
7. **Don't use misleading tactics** — cloaking, hidden landing pages, bait-and-switch
8. **Document compliance** — keep records of claims, disclosures, and consent for audit purposes
